About Me

Ahoy there, I’m Chris Flathmann and I’m a PhD student studying Human Centered Computing at Clemson University as a Research Assistant in the TRACE Research Group. After earning a BS in Computer Science from Clemson University, completing internships at Amazon and Michelin, and working as an undergraduate research assistant at Clemson, I decided that I wanted to pursue a PhD to futher explore and develop my interests in computing. Since entering the Human Centered Computing program and the TRACE Research Group I have developed a large interest in the human side of computing and the interaction between humans and the rapidly developing technology that is Artificial Intelligence. Aside from my research work I also have personal interests and hobbies like disc golf, video/tabletop games, movies (mainly sci-fi and Rom-Com), and mechanical keyboards.
